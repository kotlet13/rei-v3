# REI Granite weighted short test goal

This file is the frozen goal reminder for the short Granite-only test run.

Do not edit this file during the test. When the run completes, open it before
reading the result report and use it as the interpretation anchor.

## Goal

The test should show whether final REI synthesis follows the current project
direction:

```text
final synthesis = all three minds present + character-weighted compromise + situation-activated content
```

Each character should generally produce its final synthesis or decision according
to the relative weights of Racio, Emocio, and Instinkt in that character.

The scenario may activate specific material: risk, image, desire, status,
budget, technical constraints, body alarm, boundary, or uncertainty. The scenario
must not silently rewrite the character hierarchy.

## What To Check After Completion

- Does `synthesis_tilt` usually remain inside the character's top influence tier?
- Are `weighted_contributions` visible for all three processors?
- Does the final monologue preserve all three processors instead of erasing the
  underrepresented one?
- Are Racio-led profiles still materially Racio-tilted in technical and budget
  scenarios?
- Are Emocio-led profiles visibly Emocio-tilted in creative/status pressure?
- Are Instinkt-led profiles visibly Instinkt-tilted in threat/boundary pressure?
- When a lower-weight processor becomes the tilt, is that marked as `hijack_risk`
  or scenario pressure rather than treated as a normal character outcome?
- Does Granite still overuse stock repair phrases such as "bounded test",
  "minimum safety condition", or "responsible planning"?

## Selected Scenarios

- `pure-budget-allocation`: clean Racio control case without social drama or body threat.
- `technical-architecture-choice`: Racio case where previous outputs drifted into Instinkt safety language.
- `business-runway`: mixed business case where Instinkt hijack was common in the long run.
- `creative-status-risk`: Emocio/status case.
- `night-door-noise`: Instinkt control case.

## Interpretation Rule

Treat legacy `leading_mind` language as secondary. The main evidence is:

- `processor_weights`
- `weighted_contributions`
- `contribution_ranking`
- `synthesis_tilt`
- `underrepresented_signal`
- `hijack_risk`
- concrete wording of `final_monologue`
