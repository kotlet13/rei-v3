## Core model

A person does not experience raw reality directly. The three processors — Instinkt, Emocio, and Racio — build the person's inner world. Ego lives in that world and experiences it as reality.

Ego is not one of the three processors. Ego is the "I" that inhabits the world created by the three processors. Ego cannot directly see, hear, or feel without the three processor-friends connected to body and senses.

## Consequences

- The world one believes in is a processor-built model, not direct reality.
- Each processor sees reality through its own eyes.
- All three processors have strengths and weaknesses.
- No processor is inherently superior.
- Ego can trust one, two, or all three processors with different strengths.
- Stable relations between the three processors produce character structures.
- A person should not be judged only by the content of their world; the key is which processor produced a conclusion and how.
- Only lack of alignment among processors can preserve a more impartial relation to truth, because each processor alone tends to believe its own truth.
